Uncover the Truth Behind the Data

Truth #1: It is True Green Energy

Data Source: UNECE (2021) Life Cycle Greenhouse Gas Emissions

The public often assumes wind and solar are the ultimate green solutions. But when evaluating the entire life cycle—from mining and manufacturing to operation and decommissioning—nuclear energy's carbon footprint is staggeringly low. At just 5.1 g CO₂eq/kWh, it is even cleaner than some solar PV technologies and vastly superior to fossil fuels.

Truth #2: When Nature Pauses, Who Powers the Grid?

Data Source: U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA, 2024)

Unmatched Reliability

"Capacity factor" measures how often a plant runs at maximum power over a year. Wind and solar are at the mercy of the weather, hovering between 23% and 35%.

In stark contrast, nuclear power plants achieve an incredible 92% capacity factor. They provide stable, clean baseload electricity 24/7, powering modern civilization through dark nights and windless days.

Truth #3: Beyond the Fear, Nuclear is Exceptionally Safe

Data Source: Our World in Data (Death rate per TWh of energy production)

Historical tragedies have instilled a deep fear of nuclear power. However, cold, hard statistics reveal a counterintuitive reality: even when accounting for all historical accidents, nuclear remains one of the safest energy sources humanity has ever used. Meanwhile, air pollution from fossil fuels silently claims millions of lives every single year.
